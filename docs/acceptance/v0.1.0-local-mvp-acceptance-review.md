## 2. Executive summary

The reviewed local MVP implements a fixture-only participant flow (一献 → 初添 → 踊り → 仲添 → 留添 → 開栓), deterministic recipe generation, Canvas artwork, optional Tone.js audio, and same-browser local venue publication. Current-main quality gates, 52 Vitest tests, five Playwright E2E tests, three visual test groups, and representative axe checks passed in this review.

The review does **not** establish physical-device usability, audible quality, screen-reader behavior, a subjective prototype-fidelity judgment, a human 90–120 second completion time, or a manual two-page run. More importantly, the product-spec requirements for URL/QR `sake_id` selection and a demonstrable difference between multiple sake profiles are not implemented: the local MVP always selects its single synthetic sake fixture. This is recorded in blocking [#16](https://github.com/Chasoso/memory-brewery/issues/16).

## 3. Final decision

**NOT ACCEPTED**

The local flow and its automated evidence are healthy, but FR-01 and AC-02 are required product-spec outcomes and are not met. Human manual QA is also still required. Passing automated checks is not sufficient to override either condition.

## 17. Known limitations

- One synthetic sake fixture and three synthetic land fixtures only.
- No URL/QR `sake_id`; no multiple-sake comparison.
- Venue synchronization is limited to the same browser profile and origin; different browser, device, private window, or network synchronization is unsupported.
- No cloud persistence, AWS, deployment, authentication, QR generator, or generative AI service.
- No venue audio.
- Actual sound, physical devices, physical soft keyboard, screen reader, Safari, Android, iOS, real venue display, and subjective prototype fidelity are NOT TESTED.
- No 90–120 second human completion measurement.
